Background

Ending phone record surveillance is the first step to reining in surveillance abuses by the NSA. Please join us in making this the year we stand for privacy and liberty, not secrecy and fear.

For years, the United States government has been collecting every records of every phone call you make -- when you call, who you call, how long you spoke, and how frequently you make those calls. This surveillance program affects hundreds of millions of regular citizens who have never done anything wrong, and doesn’t matter if you’re calling your next door neighbor or a family member half-way around the world.

But there’s good news: this mass surveillance program could end in the next few weeks.

A key section of the Patriot Act – Section 215 – is set to expire on June 1, 2015. This is the law that the NSA re-interpreted in complete secrecy to allow them to collect these millions of phone call records per day. With your help, we can stop Congress from simply rubber-stamping Patriot Act Section 215 -- and stop this mass suspicionless surveillance program once and for all.

Use our phone tool about to call Congress today. Urge them to end the NSA’s untargeted mass surveillance under Section 215 of the Patriot Act. Then ask your friends to do the same.
